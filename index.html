I'm a designer and technologist who works at the intersection of human-centered design and emerging technology. Having grown up across Japan, China, and the United States, I've always been drawn to the spaces between disciplines and cultures — and to the idea that good design should bridge those gaps rather than widen them. My work tends to start with people: the small frictions, overlooked needs, and moments of disconnect that technology can either deepen or quietly resolve. I care most about creating things that restore a sense of dignity and clarity to the people who use them.

My curiosity runs across design, finance, artificial intelligence, art, and history — fields that, on the surface, seem unrelated, but that I find deeply intertwined. I'm fascinated by how markets and emerging technologies reshape the way people live and make decisions, and equally by how art and history reveal the human patterns underneath that change. I see these interests less as separate hobbies than as different lenses on the same question: how systems — economic, technological, cultural — shape human experience, and how thoughtful design can make those systems more legible, more humane, and more worth trusting.
