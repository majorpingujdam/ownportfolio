Overview

BioColor Tag is a freshness-indicator label based on anthocyanin — a natural plant pigment — that reveals food spoilage through visible color change. No electronics, no battery, no app required at the point of contact: the label itself reads the food's state and communicates it instantly through color, making safe-consumption decisions effortless for consumers and retailers alike.

Experiments

Four experiments established that anthocyanin — extracted from purple cabbage — is the viable sensing medium, and paper is the optimal carrier.

01

Color reaction — dry powder

Anthocyanin and curcumin powders are chemically stable but change color far too slowly to be usable. Not applicable.

02

Liquid extraction

Purple cabbage → cut → boil → anthocyanin solution. Solution form is reactive and workable.

03

Material adhesion

Anthocyanins color both paper and fabric well, but paper-based materials are more advantageous for industrial application. pH tests with acids (white vinegar, lemon water, hair conditioner) and alkalis (shampoo, laundry detergent, baking soda) produced significant color changes within five minutes.

04

Meat as test subject — key result

Test paper placed directly on beef tracked decomposition from Fresh → 1h → 3h → 6h → 9h → 12h → 18h → 24h. Color shift: purple → grey-green. Rotting meat releases volatile compounds (hydrogen sulfide, ammonia); high ammonia concentration drives the visible color change.

Key insight: anthocyanins are stable as dry powder but highly reactive in solution. To detect decay gases they must be kept in a moist state — which became the central design constraint.

Product Design

Four-layer structure · inspired by diaper technology

Layer 1 · Interface

Observation Layer

Hydrophobic transparent film. The visible window the consumer reads.

Layer 2 · Signal

Time-Sensing Layer

pH-responsive pigment (anthocyanin). Changes color in response to decay gases.

Layer 3 · Core

Moisture-Retaining Layer

Hydrogels maintain the moist environment that keeps the anthocyanin reactive.

Layer 4 · Base

Isolation Layer

PE film provides outer waterproofing and prevents external contamination.

App & Scenario

The companion app lets users scan a product for freshness level, product-information traceability, and purchase history — adding a digital layer to the physical label.

A boy reaches for strawberries. The label has turned dark grey-green. His mother stops him — the tiny label was quietly protecting his health.

Summary and Reflections
Before

"Best-before dates are guesses printed at the factory."

After

"The label reads the food in real time and tells you the truth."

The project demonstrated that natural molecules can do sensing work that electronic systems have failed to make accessible at scale. Through interdisciplinary design — chemistry, material science, industrial manufacturing — BioColor Tag offers a path toward smart packaging that is low-cost, biodegradable, and universally readable without any device.

Future directions include using anthocyanin tags to power dynamic supermarket pricing indicators, adjusting price in real-time as freshness declines.
