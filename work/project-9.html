Overview

Bio-Fracta Stamp is an ethical bio-designed wearable — a transdermal patch adhesive bandage that leverages fractal patterns generated by Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). By training a GAN on 92 curated fractal images, the project produces beautiful, nature-inspired patterns that can be printed onto drug-delivery patches, transforming a clinical tool into something children actually want to wear.

Brief
The Problem

Needle anxiety in children

Pediatric patients frequently experience anxiety and discomfort with traditional needle-based medication delivery — creating distress for both child and caregiver.

The Opportunity

Transdermal + fractal beauty

Transdermal patches bypass needles entirely. By covering them in GAN-generated fractal patterns children find beautiful, the patch becomes something to look forward to rather than fear.

The Insight

Kids prefer nature's fractals

Research shows that by age 3, children already prefer nature's fractal patterns. This project asks: what if we let kids pick their own fractal patch design — turning medication into play?

Inspiration & Sputnik Moment

As someone who loves exploring nature and grows fungi as a hobby, Owen was captivated by the fractal patterns created by slime molds — intricate, branching veins formed as they forage for nutrients. During fractal research, a paper revealed that children as young as 3 already prefer nature's fractal patterns. The leap came immediately: fractal patterns → blood vessels → why not create something that helps youngsters release stress through beautiful, customizable designs? (Owen is also quite scared of needles himself.)

GAN Training Process

Starting from scratch, Owen built a GAN environment using Google Colab, standing on the shoulders of resources from Derrick Schultz and Jeff Heaton, then modifying and combining them to work with a local Google Drive folder structure.

Pipeline · six steps

  1. 01Collect 92 fractal images (Shutterstock / Unsplash)
  2. 02Resize all to 512×512 px in Photoshop
  3. 03Mount to Google Drive, set folder paths
  4. 04Install JAX + CUDA / downgrade PyTorch
  5. 05Train StyleGAN3 on image dataset
  6. 06Pick 5 favourite output patterns

Prototype & Future Materials

With five favourite fractal patterns selected from the GAN output, Owen explored what sustainable and body-safe materials could carry the patterns as a real transdermal patch — identifying three promising pathways.

Option A

Biodegradable Polymers

Cellulose, chitosan, or polylactic acid (PLA) derived from renewable sources. Reduces environmental impact while providing suitable drug-release profiles; degrades harmlessly after use.

Option B

Natural Hydrogels

Alginate, gelatin, or agarose-based hydrogels offer biocompatibility and a comfortable, flexible matrix. Can be loaded with active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) and applied directly to skin.

Option C

Nanostructured Materials

Nanocellulose or plant-derived nanofibers provide improved mechanical properties and enhanced drug-loading capacity — with controlled release and sustainable production.

Wild card idea: Egg outer membrane as a biodegradable patch substrate — biocompatible, flexible, and a material that would otherwise be discarded as kitchen waste. If made edible, it could also reduce risk if young children accidentally ingest it.
