01 Overview

At Beijing Ai-Robotics (鍖椾含澶ц壘鏈哄櫒浜?, my design team was focused on making the exoskeleton itself better 鈥?a natural priority for a hardware company. As I talked to users across the country, a pattern kept surfacing: the device worked well in the hospital, but often went quiet once people got home. What broke wasn't the hardware 鈥?it was the support around it the moment the therapist stepped away.

That reframed the question for me 鈥?from how to improve the device to how to keep rehab going after the patient goes home 鈥?and pointed toward designing a clinic-to-home service that holds together across that gap.

05 The Insight

The therapist holds the operating knowledge; the family does not. In hospital this is invisible 鈥?the therapist silently fills the gap. Once home rehab begins and they step away, the device goes unused.

AiWalker's core gap is not in the hardware 鈥?it's in service continuity across the clinic-to-home handoff.
