Overview

DialogueGuardian is a back-end application module designed to be integrated into future android police units. Using Natural Language Processing and sentiment analysis in Jupyter Notebook, it analyzes real-time conversations between android officers and civilians — then guides officers toward ethical, de-escalating communication. The goal: remain professional under pressure, protect civilian rights, and minimize tension before it becomes force.

Inspiration

As a reader of O. Henry's novels, Owen found that "The Cop and the Anthem" carries a deeper social truth: a single conversation with a person holding a badge can redirect the entire course of someone's life. As a student in NYC, Owen regularly witnessed police-civilian clashes — arguments, physical confrontations, and the power asymmetry underneath. With AI and robotics advancing toward android officers, the question became urgent: how should a machine talk to a human under pressure, while respecting rights and minimizing harm?

Creating the Dataset

Owen collected ~105 real sentences and responses from police body-camera recordings sourced from YouTube channels PoliceActivity and Law & Crime. These were split into two template sets:

Template Set 1 — Officer

How officers typically communicate — drawn from real body-cam footage to capture authentic language patterns and escalation triggers.

Template Set 2 — Citizen Profiles

Responses classified by profession — so the analyzer can tailor officer replies to each civilian context.

Sentiment Analysis Process

Pipeline · six steps

  1. 01Collect 105 real sentences from body-cam footage
  2. 02Build profession-based citizen response templates
  3. 03Use Random module to generate 4-sentence conversations
  4. 04Load NLTK + pull VADER lexicon for sentiment scoring
  5. 05Analyze overall conversation sentiment (positive / neutral / negative)
  6. 06Output guidance directive to the android officer

Owen chose NLTK over spaCy for its modularity and educational depth — using the vader_lexicon specifically for its strength in social/conversational text. The Random module generates variation across conversation runs, producing different outcomes each time the cell is executed.

Conversation Output & Results
Positive Sentiment

Keep the trend

Officer is directed to maintain the current communication approach — the conversation is going well and trust is building.

Neutral Sentiment

Monitor closely

Situation is stable but not warm — officer is flagged to watch for shifts and stay measured in tone.

Negative Sentiment

Re-direct conversation

Officer is prompted to de-escalate immediately, with profession-specific language suggested to rebuild trust.

Example — Lawyer profile: "As professionals, I believe we can find a resolution that respects everyone's rights and ensures a safe outcome. Can we find common ground and agree on steps we can take to mitigate the tension?"
Summary & What's Next
Before

"Android officers have no way to assess conversation tone or adjust communication in real time — every interaction is a black box."

After

"DialogueGuardian reads the room — positive, neutral, or negative — and guides the android toward responses that respect rights and reduce tension."

Next steps include embedding DialogueGuardian into an interactive web interface, expanding de-escalation templates per profession, and training on a larger, more diverse dataset. The core insight — that profession context should shape how authority speaks to people — is both the project's strength and its most promising direction for growth.
